1969 - ARPANET

The internet was born as ARPANET, a U.S. military research project that connected computers.

ARPANET was the first network to implement the TCP/IP protocol suite, which became the foundation of the modern internet.

ARPANET initially connected four university computers: UCLA, Stanford, UC Santa Barbara, and the University of Utah. This small network quickly grew, connecting more institutions and paving the way for the modern internet.

Key Features of ARPANET

  • Packet Switching: ARPANET used packet switching to send data in small packets, which could take different paths to their destination.
  • Decentralization: Unlike traditional communication systems, ARPANET was decentralized, meaning there was no single point of failure.
  • Protocols: ARPANET developed protocols like NCP (Network Control Protocol) and later TCP/IP, which are still used today.

Impact on Modern Internet

ARPANET's development laid the groundwork for the global internet. Its innovations in networking, protocols, and decentralization are still fundamental to how the internet operates today.
