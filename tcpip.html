1983 - TCP/IP

TCP/IP became the standard, allowing different networks to communicate.

It is the fundamental protocol suite that underpins the internet.

What is TCP/IP?

TCP/IP stands for Transmission Control Protocol/Internet Protocol. It is a set of rules that governs how data is transmitted over the internet.

Components of TCP/IP

  • TCP: Ensures data is sent and received accurately. It breaks data into packets, sends them, and reassembles them at the destination.
  • IP: Handles addressing and routing. It ensures packets are sent to the correct destination.

Importance of TCP/IP

TCP/IP's introduction allowed different types of networks to communicate, leading to the interconnected global network we have today. It is the backbone of internet communication, enabling everything from web browsing to email.
